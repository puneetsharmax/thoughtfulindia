We went quiet for a while.

The world didn't.

When we last wrote regularly, India was debating demonetisation and the first wave of GST chaos. The world was absorbing a Trump presidency nobody had fully modelled. China was still the unstoppable juggernaut. The Gulf was simmering, not boiling.

Much has changed. Some things haven't changed nearly enough.

India is faster now — its economy, its ambitions, its contradictions. The country that ranked 142nd on the World Bank's Ease of Doing Business index in 2014 broke into the top 63 by the time the index was retired. The UPI payments architecture processes more digital transactions than Visa. The space agency lands on the moon's south pole and the world genuinely applauds.

And yet. And yet the state universities are in crisis. The manufacturing base that should have arrived still hasn't. The conversation about India's place in the world remains either too triumphalist or too self-flagellating — rarely the thing it should be: clear-eyed.

That's the gap we came back to fill.

ThoughtfulIndia was never a news aggregator and it isn't going to become one now. What we're here to do is take the best of what's happening in the world — the geopolitics, the economics, the long arcs of demographic change and technological disruption — and run it through a serious Indian lens.

Not a nationalist lens. Not an apologetic lens. An informed, unsentimental, India-first lens.

The noise has gotten louder since we were last here. The algorithm rewards outrage. The 24-hour news cycle has become a 24-second cycle. Opinions arrive before facts do.

We're back to cut through it.

Welcome back to ThoughtfulIndia.
