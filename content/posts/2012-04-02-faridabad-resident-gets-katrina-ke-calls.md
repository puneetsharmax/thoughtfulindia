The confusion that arises from shared names took an unusual and somewhat charming turn for a Faridabad resident who found himself receiving a steady stream of phone calls intended for Bollywood superstar Katrina Kaif — the calls arriving because his phone number was apparently similar to or identical with what some of her fans believed to be hers.

The phenomenon illustrates something about celebrity culture and the specific intensity of Bollywood fandom: that a significant number of people genuinely attempt to contact film stars by phone, whether through numbers shared in unverified corners of the internet or through elaborate guesswork.

For the Faridabad resident, the experience ranged from amusing to exhausting, depending on the caller. Some were simply confused and apologetic when it became clear they had the wrong person. Others, apparently unwilling to accept that Katrina Kaif was not available on this particular number, were persistent or creative in their responses.

Stories of this type — regular people receiving misdirected fame, whether through number mix-ups, name coincidences, or the occasional social media account confusion — have a particular texture in India, where the gap between celebrity and ordinary life can be enormous while the emotional investment in stars can be intensely personal.

The Faridabad resident reportedly handled the situation with good humor, occasionally playing along, occasionally explaining the error, and eventually deciding that the story was worth telling precisely because it captured something true about the peculiar dynamics of fan culture.

Katrina Kaif, for her part, was presumably unaware of the entire episode — as, presumably, she is of most of the attempts to reach her by unconventional means.
