**Some resources for the young minds**

 [Cricketmag.com.](http://www.cricketmag.com/ProductList.aspx?type=M) : Various choices of digital and print magazine selection can be found here. Prices range from $17 to $50 for a monthly subscription. Magazines are fun, exploratory for the kids in the age group of 5-10 year olds. Since 1973 cricket magazines have satiated the curiosity of kids in Arts, Science and History.

 

[Fatbrain.com](https://www.fatbraintoys.com/toys/toys_by_ages/boys/top_picks_6.cfm) – This website features toys and other educational products for all age kids. Books, Snap circuits, Imaginative Toys, Puzzles-if you are lost for an inspiration –this is one-stop-site to fuel the minds of the young in your house.

 

[The Magic School Bus Subscription](http://www.theyoungscientistsclub.com/themagicschoolbus/): The yearly subscription will take the kids on a different journey with Ms. Frizzle each month. Each month your child will receive a different science kit in the mail enticing him to explore an area of science. Most appropriate for 5-10 year-olds. The whole year subscription costs $240.

 

[Ck12.org](Ck12.org): This not for profit provides textbooks and digital help for K-12 kids. Lessons are available for kindergarteners to SAT preparations.

 

[Read Theory](http://readtheory.org/): This website specializes in improving the reading comprehension skills of the kids ranging from grade1-grade12. Right now the website is free for the students to use. It’s still in beta stage or development mode. But we have not come across any bugs.
