This week's ThoughtfulIndia Find of the Week highlights a local creative entrepreneur who has turned a love of crafting into a genuine community resource: Bobbie Lee, founder of Scrap & Craft Workshop in the western suburbs of Chicago.

Bobbie Lee runs hands-on craft workshops for children between the ages of 7 and 12, with a focus on projects that are genuinely made by hand rather than assembled from kits. The workshops rotate through techniques — paper crafting, collage, fabric work, basic sewing, and seasonal projects tied to holidays and special occasions — giving participants both variety and the chance to develop real skills over multiple sessions.

What distinguishes Bobbie Lee's workshops from standard craft classes is the emphasis on creativity over instruction. Children are given materials and shown basic techniques, but then encouraged to develop their own approaches. The result, according to parents who have enrolled their children, is work that actually reflects individual imagination rather than mass-produced uniformity.

The workshops are designed to be small — typically no more than eight children per session — which allows for genuine individual attention. Bobbie Lee teaches each session herself and adapts the curriculum based on what the group is interested in and what they've already mastered.

For parents looking for enrichment activities that develop fine motor skills, creative thinking, and a sense of accomplishment without requiring expensive equipment or prior experience, Scrap & Craft Workshop is worth investigating. Details about upcoming sessions and enrollment can be obtained through direct inquiry.
