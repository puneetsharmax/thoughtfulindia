Schools Superintendent Jennifer Gray said she expects school board members to meet within the next two weeks to determine if disciplinary action is warranted for a teacher who made slurs about first lady Michelle Obama and homosexuals.

Gray said the school board likely will meet in a special session before Feb. 14 regarding Lauderdale County High School head football coach Bob Grisham’s remarks which were made in front of students earlier in the week. The discussion revolved around the country’s problems as viewed by Grisham.

Gray said the investigation into the coach’s remarks is progressing. The next scheduled board meeting is Feb. 14, but Gray said the board likely will make a decision on how to proceed before that time.

Grisham’s teaching status has not been changed since the remarks were made Monday. Grisham said he misspoke in a debate-style discussion.

Gray said she has not spoken to Grisham since a brief discussion with him when she learned of the remarks. School officials said Grisham has been tending to personal business and has not been back at school this week.

A student made an audio recording of Grisham making disparaging remarks about first lady Michelle Obama and gays. He referred to the first lady at one point as “fat butt Michelle Obama,” He also refers multiple times on the 1 minute 24-second tape to his disdain of “queers.”

The student who made the tape has not been identified.

Under Alabama’s new Students First Act, the school board must be involved in any decision regarding employee discipline, including suspension or any type of leave.

Gray must make a recommendation about what disciplinary action, if any, is warranted.

Grisham has been employed by the school system since 1991 and has been head football coach at Lauderdale County High School for the past five years. His team advanced to the state semifinals this past season.
