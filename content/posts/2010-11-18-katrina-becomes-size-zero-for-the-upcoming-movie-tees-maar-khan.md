Katrina Kaif has always been known for her toned physique, but for her role in the upcoming comedy *Tees Maar Khan*, the actress reportedly pushed herself to reach size zero — the ultra-slim body standard that had already swept through Bollywood following Kareena Kapoor's transformation for *Tashan*.

The pressure on leading actresses to conform to a specific body type has been a persistent issue in the Indian film industry. Where Bollywood heroines once celebrated curves, by the late 2000s a new aesthetic had taken hold — and Katrina's reported attempt to slim down further for a comedic role raised eyebrows among fans and health commentators alike.

*Tees Maar Khan*, directed by Farah Khan and starring Akshay Kumar alongside Katrina, was one of the most anticipated releases of the 2010 holiday season. The film blended slapstick with heist comedy, and Katrina's item number "Sheila Ki Jawani" became a cultural phenomenon that overshadowed everything else in the film — generating massive radio play and YouTube views months before the movie even hit theaters.

The size-zero debate in Bollywood reflects a broader global conversation about body image, media influence, and the health of young women who look to film stars as role models. Nutritionists and fitness experts in India repeatedly cautioned that extreme weight loss achieved through crash diets could lead to serious health consequences, including bone density loss and hormonal imbalances.

Katrina ultimately delivered a performance and a dance sequence that became one of the defining pop culture moments of 2010, regardless of what any scale said.
