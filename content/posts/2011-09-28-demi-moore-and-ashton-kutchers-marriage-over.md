After months of tabloid speculation and a very public unraveling, Demi Moore confirmed what many had suspected: her six-year marriage to Ashton Kutcher was over. Moore filed for divorce, citing irreconcilable differences, bringing to an end one of Hollywood's most scrutinized relationships.

The couple had been together since 2003, marrying in 2005 in a ceremony that surprised many given their 15-year age gap. Moore was 43 at the time; Kutcher was 27. Initially, they seemed to defy the odds — both were committed to the relationship, appeared frequently together, and Kutcher developed close bonds with Moore's three daughters from her previous marriage to Freddie Moore.

The marriage began showing strain publicly in late 2011 when reports surfaced that Kutcher had been unfaithful on the couple's sixth anniversary. Kutcher subsequently separated from Moore, and she was hospitalized briefly afterward — reportedly suffering from stress-related issues. The split became tabloid fodder.

Both had kept largely quiet about the specifics, but Moore later spoke about the toll the relationship had taken on her sense of self-worth, the pressure of maintaining her appearance as a woman in a relationship with a significantly younger man, and her struggles with substance abuse during the marriage's collapse.

For Kutcher, life moved on quickly. He had been co-starring with Mila Kunis on "That '70s Show" for years, and the two began dating after Moore and Kutcher separated. They eventually married in 2015 and have children together.

The Moore-Kutcher marriage had been a cultural fixture — proof, many said, that age-gap relationships could work in Hollywood. Its end was a reminder that fame and chemistry don't insulate any couple from the ordinary pressures that break relationships apart.
