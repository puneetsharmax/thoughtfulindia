Nobody likes doing their income tax. It is stressful, confusing, and for many people, deeply boring. But ignoring it or getting it wrong costs money — sometimes a lot of it.

That's why ThoughtfulIndia's Find of the Week this week is Gary L. Anderson, a Naperville-based tax consultant with over two decades of experience helping individuals, small business owners, and immigrants navigate the U.S. tax system.

Anderson's practice stands out for one key reason: he specializes in working with clients who have income and assets in multiple countries. For Indian-Americans filing returns that involve overseas property, foreign bank accounts, or income from India, this kind of expertise is invaluable. Mistakes on forms like the FBAR (FinCEN 114) or FATCA disclosures can lead to steep IRS penalties — and most general tax preparers simply aren't equipped to handle them.

He also handles Schedule C filings for self-employed consultants and small business owners, a common situation in the Indian-American professional community where many people do contract or freelance work in addition to their regular jobs.

Anderson's office offers consultations for first-time clients, and he has a reputation for being patient with people who feel overwhelmed by the U.S. tax code. His rates are competitive for the level of specialization he brings, and past clients describe him as someone who actually explains what he's filing and why — rare in an industry where most preparers just run the numbers and hand you the forms.

If your taxes involve complexity — international income, rental properties, stock options, or multiple income streams — a specialist like Gary L. Anderson is worth the investment. Getting it right the first time saves far more than his fee.
