Whistleblower site WikiLeaks has begun to release what is estimated to be some 400,000 classified military documents related to U.S. operations in Iraq. The papers reportedly offer new details about civilian deaths, as well as the mistreatment of Iraqi insurgents.
Many of the documents are what the military calls "significant action" reports
