The Naperville Public Library has long served as a cultural hub for the community, and few of its programs have cultivated a more devoted following than the Retro Reel screening series curated by Sreepathy. Now in its 23rd volume, the series continues its tradition of bringing classic and critically acclaimed films to audiences who appreciate cinema beyond the multiplex.

Volume 23 puts the spotlight on M. Night Shyamalan's 1999 psychological thriller *The Sixth Sense*, a film that redefined the genre and remains one of the most discussed twist endings in movie history. Bruce Willis stars as Malcolm Crowe, a child psychologist who takes on the case of a troubled young boy — played by a revelatory Haley Joel Osment — who whispers the now-iconic line: "I see dead people." What unfolds is a taut, atmospheric story about grief, guilt, and the haunting weight of unfinished business.

*The Sixth Sense* was a cultural phenomenon upon release, earning six Academy Award nominations including Best Picture and Best Director. More than two decades later, it holds up as a masterclass in misdirection — a film best experienced knowing as little as possible, and best re-experienced knowing everything.

Sreepathy's Retro Reel series has introduced Naperville audiences to an eclectic range of titles across its previous volumes, from Hollywood classics to international gems. Volume 23 continues that curatorial spirit, pairing an accessible crowd-pleaser with deeper resonance for those willing to look twice.

The screening is open to library cardholders and film enthusiasts alike. Check the Naperville Public Library's events calendar for dates, times, and any registration requirements. Whether you are discovering *The Sixth Sense* for the first time or revisiting it with fresh eyes, Volume 23 promises to be an evening well spent.
