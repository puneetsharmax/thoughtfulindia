Aishwarya Rai Bachchan and Abhishek Bachchan welcomed their first child, a baby girl, bringing one of Bollywood's most celebrated families cause for widespread celebration. The birth was greeted with enormous public interest — understandably, given that the baby is the daughter of two film stars and the granddaughter of Amitabh Bachchan, widely regarded as the greatest living actor in Indian cinema.

Aishwarya, a former Miss World and one of the most recognizable faces in global cinema, had announced her pregnancy earlier in the year. The news had been received with the kind of enthusiastic public attention that is reserved in India for the Bachchan family, whose lives are treated with an intensity of interest that has few equivalents elsewhere.

Abhishek Bachchan confirmed the birth and expressed the family's joy. Amitabh Bachchan, for his part, conveyed his happiness through his blog and public statements — the patriarch of a family that had learned, over decades, to share its milestones with a public that considered itself a stakeholder in their story.

The birth rekindled a conversation that had surfaced during Aishwarya's pregnancy: the scrutiny of a woman's body during and after pregnancy, the expectations placed on a public figure to look a certain way, and the double standard that applies differently to women and men in the spotlight. Aishwarya would later face significant commentary about her post-pregnancy appearance — commentary that drew widespread criticism as intrusive and sexist.

For the moment, however, the news was simply what it was: the birth of a child, welcomed by a family, and celebrated by a nation that had long since adopted the Bachchans as something more than entertainers.
