Esha Deol's mehendi ceremony drew Bollywood's social set to Mumbai for an afternoon of color, tradition, and the particular celebrity culture that surrounds star-family weddings in India.

The daughter of veteran actors Hema Malini and Dharmendra, Esha married businessman Bharat Takhtani in a traditional Hindu ceremony in June 2012. The pre-wedding mehendi celebration was an occasion for the film fraternity to gather, and the guest list read like a who's who of both generations of Bollywood.

The color and style choices at Bollywood events have become a subject of serious fashion commentary in India, with celebrity outfits analyzed and ranked with the same attention given to red carpet appearances at the Oscars or Cannes. Anita Hassanandani arrived in peacock blue, Esha herself wore a traditional orange and gold lehenga, and the elder generation—including Hema Malini—held to classic colors befitting the occasion.

The ceremony itself was celebratory in the classical sense: musicians played, relatives danced, and Esha's hands were decorated with intricate mehendi patterns that would take hours to set. The event had the warmth of a genuine family celebration despite its scale.

The Deol family occupies a particular place in Hindi film history. Dharmendra was one of the defining action stars of the 1970s; Hema Malini the "Dream Girl" of an era. Their daughter's wedding was, for many fans of that generation, a sentimental moment—watching the children of beloved stars step into their own adult lives.

For Esha, who had a modest film career, the wedding marked a conscious choice to step away from the industry and build a family. It was, by all accounts, a joyful occasion.
