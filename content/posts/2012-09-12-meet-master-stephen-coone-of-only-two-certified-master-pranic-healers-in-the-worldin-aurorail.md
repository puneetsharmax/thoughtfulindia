Among the thousands of martial arts instructors teaching across North America, Master Stephen Co holds a distinction shared by almost no one: he is one of only two people in the United States certified as a master instructor in Pranic Healing, an energy-based healing modality developed by the late Grand Master Choa Kok Sui, who spent decades synthesizing and systematizing esoteric healing traditions from across Asia into a teachable framework.

Co, who has taught Pranic Healing for more than two decades, came to the practice through a background in martial arts — specifically in Chi Kung, the Chinese practice of cultivating and directing what practitioners describe as the life energy that underlies physical health and vitality. His path illustrates the connection between martial arts traditions and healing practices that has deep roots in Asian philosophy, where the same subtle energy that martial artists seek to harness for fighting is understood to be the same energy that healers direct for health.

Pranic Healing operates on the premise that the human body has an energy field that can be assessed and adjusted, that disease manifests in the energy body before it appears in the physical body, and that trained practitioners can accelerate healing by removing congested energy and projecting fresh energy into depleted areas.

The modality has attracted both devoted practitioners who report significant results and skeptics who note that controlled clinical evidence for energy healing remains limited and contested.

What is undisputed is that Master Co has become one of the most sought-after teachers of the practice in the United States, drawing students who range from the spiritually curious to healthcare professionals interested in integrative approaches.

His teaching emphasizes character development and ethical living as prerequisites for effective healing work — a dimension of the practice that he describes as inseparable from the technical skills.
