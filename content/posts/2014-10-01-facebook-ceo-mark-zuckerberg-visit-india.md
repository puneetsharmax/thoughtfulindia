Zuckerberg will be on India from Oct.9-10 to attend and address the summit Internet.org. Internet.org ‘s aim is to make Internet affordable to the 5 billion people across the globe.![Screen-Shot-2013-09-30-at-11.47.59-AM-730x403](/images/2014/10/Screen-Shot-2013-09-30-at-11.47.59-AM-730x4031.png)

Internet.org is a group that Zuckerberg founded along with mobile-device makers Samsung, Nokia, and Qualcomm to promote “Knowledge Economy”.  [Started in August, Internet.org](http://thenextweb.com/facebook/2013/09/30/mark-zuckerberg-how-to-make-the-internet-100x-more-affordable-by-lowering-costs-and-reducing-data/) is Zuckerberg’s second major effort to help advance technology, coming months after the [launch of his political action committee, FWD.us](http://thenextweb.com/insider/2013/04/11/fwd-us-new-silicon-valley-lobby-launches/). Facebook estimates that 5 billion people, or nearly two-thirds of the world, are without Internet access, and it wants to make sure that knowledge is better shared in this global community.

 

 

Watch his video on linking the 5 billion people of the world and making Internet more affordable. [youtube]http://youtu.be/AdXwthh-xLQ[/youtube]

 

Zuckerberg will be meeting PM Narendra Modi and some other dignitaries as well. These past months Sheryl Sandberg, Jeff Bezos were the other high profile CEOs to be visiting India.
